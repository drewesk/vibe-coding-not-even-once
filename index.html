Coding with a systems thinking approach

What does this mean? What really is systems thinking. Well let's compare it to medicine where many doctors specialize in their dicipline but have to punt you from doctor to doctor if they cannot connect the dots with other areas of modern medicine. A holistic doctor has one advantage in that they have the ability to go to medical school and decide how to approach the human body as a complete map. They may not have the benefit of the latest tools and technology but atleast they can be very effective atleast in finding negative habits and patterns that many other doctors overlook. But what if you could have the benefit of all the modern technology and also approach medicine with a connect the dots mentality, it would be less of an assembly line and they would have all of the tools to be able to diagnose and find the root cause quickly. So systems thinking is being able to look at technology from a birds eye view and at the same time be able to fufill deficencies and be able to look across diperate methodologies but still connect the dots to create next level systems, software, hardware configurations, and networking to fit the needs of the future. With the increase of advanced technologies across europe and asia, specifically talking about Russia, China, India, North Korea, Israel, and a few other countries and regions responsible for creating some of the most advanced technologies and malware. The US whether we agree with the rate of advancement or not is competing on the global market to produce the next level of CPU manufacturing and tech-infrastructure to both keep our military and espionage hackers (whether the US admits to having some of the best hackers at their finger tips) well equipped to stay ahead.
